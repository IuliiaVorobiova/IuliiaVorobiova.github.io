Role of service design

Self-management

SD increases service-provider’s empathy with users, motivating providers to better convey their chronic disease self-management solutions.

“Using an approach toward cognitive empathy, designers apply different methods to build up that competence and insight, enabling them to prioritize the needs of the users and make the results of the process more desirable".


Adjusting information-flow

SD provides user-centered methods to present more efficient treatment processes involving doctors and patients by simplifying and adjusting information-flow.

“In practice, then, the flow of health care work activities is often much less linear than it is in other areas, with roles much more flexible depend and overlapping, and distinctions between steps much fuzzier than the formalized PCIS ( patient care information system) models would have it".

Collaboration

SD helps cross-organizational staffs better collaborate through being more aware of their own roles by introducing design thinking and methods.

“Rather than bringing design into an organization, service designers can support organizations in advancing its very own design practices by exploring alternative design approaches and by developing a design attitude (Michlewski, 2008)".
